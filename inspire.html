Who was John F. Kennedy?

John F. Kennedy, the 35th President of the United States, served from 1961 until his assassination in 1963.

A charismatic leader, Kennedy inspired the nation with his vision for progress, famously declaring, “Ask not what your country can do for you—ask what you can do for your country.” His presidency was marked by pivotal events, including the Cuban Missile Crisis, the establishment of the Peace Corps, the Space Race, and early advancements in civil rights. Despite his short time in office, Kennedy's legacy endures as a symbol of youthful optimism and bold leadership.
